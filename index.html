Welcome to the College of Information, Entertainment, and Communication

Established in 2019, the College of Information Technology, Entertainment, and Communication (CITEC) is one of the newest additions to UBLC. It originated as a small department offering the Bachelor of Science in Information Technology and played a pivotal role as one of UBLC’s pioneer programs back in 2011. The introduction of the Bachelor in Multimedia Arts program paved the way for the establishment of this thriving college.

CITEC is committed to nurturing critical thinkers, fostering research-oriented minds, and empowering individuals with creativity and innovation. We aim to shape our students into skilled professionals who can analyze complex organizational challenges, design innovative solutions, and contribute to technological advancements in the fields of information technology, entertainment, and communication.

At CITEC, we believe in unleashing the full potential of our students through experiential learning. Our dedicated and highly qualified faculty members serve as mentors, inspiring each student to achieve their best. We provide an environment conducive to learning because, at UBLC, CITEC is where technology meets creativity.

Discover boundless opportunities for growth, creativity, and technological excellence at the College of Information Technology, Entertainment, and Communication. Welcome to a dynamic learning experience!”.

Academic Programs Offered

  • **Bachelor of Science in Information Technology (BSIT):** Focuses on computer utilization, software development, network administration, and managing IT infrastructure for business solutions.
  • **Bachelor of Science in Multimedia Arts (BMMA):** Equips students with knowledge and skills in multimedia design, production, and development, covering areas like animation, graphic design, web content, and video production.

CITEC Academic and Project Requirements

Capstone Project Requirements

The Capstone Project or Thesis is a mandatory final-year requirement for both the BSIT and BMMA programs, designed to synthesize all acquired knowledge and skills.

  • **Phase 1: Proposal and Feasibility Study** (Completed in Capstone 1 course) - Submission of project title, statement of the problem, literature review, and detailed project methodology.
  • **Phase 2: Development and Documentation** (Completed in Capstone 2 course) - Implementation of the working system (BSIT) or final creative product (BMMA), followed by the creation of a comprehensive technical/research paper.
  • **Phase 3: Final Oral Defense** - Formal presentation and defense of the project before a panel of experts and faculty.
  • **Final Output:** Must be a viable solution (BSIT) or a high-quality creative work (BMMA) that addresses a real-world need.

Minimum Marks Needed for Continuation

To ensure academic excellence and timely graduation, students must adhere to the following minimum standards (Note: Specific grades vary per semester and college policy; these are general university guidelines):

Requirement Minimum Mark Policy Detail
**Minimum Passing Mark (Per Subject)** **75%** (or Grade Point Equivalent of **3.0** or **P** for Pass) Any grade below this (e.g., 5.0 or 69%) is considered a failure and requires re-enrollment.
**General Weighted Average (GWA)** Minimum **2.75** (or specified GWA equivalent) Students must maintain the retention GWA per semester/year. Failure to do so may result in academic probation.
**Major Subject Minimum** Minimum **2.0** (or higher, e.g., 85%) For core professional subjects, a higher minimum may be enforced to proceed to more advanced, prerequisite-dependent courses.
**Maximum Failed Units** Not more than **9 Units** of failure per academic year. Exceeding the maximum allowed failed units can lead to academic disqualification or mandatory course shifting.
